[![](https://web.archive.org/web/20111010064806im_/http://2.bp.blogspot.com/_nmjS6fd9kfU/TFZMVVg04rI/AAAAAAAAAMw/EYgfVPiOVZ8/s400/folder.jpg)](https://web.archive.org/web/20111010064806/http://2.bp.blogspot.com/_nmjS6fd9kfU/TFZMVVg04rI/AAAAAAAAAMw/EYgfVPiOVZ8/s1600/folder.jpg)"This score is for "The Visualz Anthology"[a 4.5 out of 5], a compilation that isn't listed here on RYM but which includes this EP and several other tracks. I have no clue how the artists on Fondle 'Em aren't more known amongst fans of underground hip-hop or East Coast hip-hop, but it certainly isn't because of a lack of quality. The beats are amazing. Boom-bap with mysterious, liquidy jazz samples and the two MCs trading clever rhymes and lines over them having what sounds like a ton of fun. The chemistry that the two have with each other is an undeniable part of this record's success as well, perfectly exemplified in the epic "A Day Like Any Other", a track that would normally cause me to recoil in horror due to the thought of an eleven minute hip-hop song. However, the multiple beat changes (every single one is dope), and the way the MCs finish each others' lines and in general feed off the other makes it seem shorter than a freakin' grindcore song.  
  
And with that, my friends, I'll leave you to listen to this album, because clearly you're a fool if you aren't attempting to acquire it already." -- Me.
