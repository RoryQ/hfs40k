[![](https://web.archive.org/web/20111010085349im_/http://4.bp.blogspot.com/_nmjS6fd9kfU/SklurQzj_UI/AAAAAAAAACs/jSvDk3f4yUA/s400/folder.jpg)](https://web.archive.org/web/20111010085349/http://4.bp.blogspot.com/_nmjS6fd9kfU/SklurQzj_UI/AAAAAAAAACs/jSvDk3f4yUA/s1600-h/folder.jpg)One of Andrew's 60's Blue Note classics, ranking up there with Black Fire and Point of Departure, this release finds him in a quartet setting with Bobby Hutcherson on vibraphone, Richard Davis on bass, and Elvin Jones on drums. Bobby Hutcherson's unique sound on the vibraphones helped to define the sound of many of Blue Note's best avant-garde albums, from Eric Dolphy's Out to Lunch to Grachan Moncur III's Evolution. The haunting sound that the vibes have when they're left to reverberate is crucial to the atmospheres of all of these albums.  
  
As always, Elvin Jones provides his trademark ebbing and flowing percussion accompanient, which keeps the music moving and prevents anyone from ever being allowed to set into a rut for too long, which keeps the music continuously fresh. Bassist Richard Davis strikes up an excellent rapport with Jones, and provides interesting solos at a few points in the album. A great release, and one of the reasons why Hill is on the top of my list of jazz artists.
