[![](https://web.archive.org/web/20111010124053im_/http://4.bp.blogspot.com/_JdyrURA7JYI/S1UoUgJCDGI/AAAAAAAAANo/tELS7nM3pWQ/s400/herbiesextant.PNG)](https://web.archive.org/web/20111010124053/http://4.bp.blogspot.com/_JdyrURA7JYI/S1UoUgJCDGI/AAAAAAAAANo/tELS7nM3pWQ/s1600-h/herbiesextant.PNG)When most people hear the name "Herbie Hancock" they either think "oh yeah, he made Head Hunters, what a good album" or "didn't that guy win a grammy a few years back? I thought he was dead."  
  
Yeah, well fuck you.  
  
Sextant, arguably Herbie's most underrated release is what you get from melding Bitches Brew-esque funk with Sun Ra-esque multi-keyboard experimentation. The closest thing to "avant-funk" that isn't Miles Davis' classic. Both albums prominently feature trumpet and keyboard, with multiple percussionists filling in the background. Also, Bennie Maupin guests on both albums as bass clarinetist. Yeah, the bubbly electronics on "Rain Dance" and the mellow funk of "Hidden Shadows" are a little aimless, but the side B composition "Hornets" is worth the download. Herbie pretty much goes all out using several different keyboard affects that swish and swirl and blast. It's one of my favorite lengthy jams ever and shouldn't be missed.
