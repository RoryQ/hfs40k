While the cover art seems weird as all hell, what lies inside, though, is an album chock full of beautiful avant-chamber pop songs with a bit of post-rocky (in the Bark Psychosis/ Talk Talk vein) influence. From the wonderful swelling strings and hook of "Prelude to 110 or 220/Women of the World" to the Laughing Stock-esque "Movie on the Way Down", this is a drop-dead gorgeous suite of songs that perfectly exemplify what an amazing knack for melody and sheer *beauty* Jim O'Rourke has, which is perfectly tempered by his tasteful forays into electronic glitchery and other experimentation. Listening is required.
