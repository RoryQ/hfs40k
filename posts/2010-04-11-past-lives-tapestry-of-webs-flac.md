[![](https://web.archive.org/web/20111010045602im_/http://3.bp.blogspot.com/_nmjS6fd9kfU/S8J-T6A7ByI/AAAAAAAAAII/SmIhOXGKbQg/s400/folder.jpg)](https://web.archive.org/web/20111010045602/http://3.bp.blogspot.com/_nmjS6fd9kfU/S8J-T6A7ByI/AAAAAAAAAII/SmIhOXGKbQg/s1600/folder.jpg)Apparently this band was formed from the ashes of the Blood Brothers, a post-hardcore band that I've heard of but never listened to. They were the second opener for The Thermals at their show on Friday night, and I enjoyed their angular and melodically dissonant -- yeah, you read that right -- guitar melodies (I'm such a sucker for this particular trait). The lead vocalist reminded me slightly of Guy Picciotto from Fugazi, which can only be a plus. Overall, solid rhythm section, good songwriting, and a very listenable and varied album. 7.5/10 from Pitchfork, for all you hip hip hipsters out there.

[Enjoy](https://web.archive.org/web/20111010045602/http://lix.in/-79a467)

Edit: Hex Takes Hold is the jam of a lifetime. This is definitely a grower. "I'M TELLLLINNN' YOUUUUUU"
