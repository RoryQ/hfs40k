In my estimation you either love The Fall or hate The Fall. It is difficult to interpret the music any differently or in any shade of grey. Some love M.E. Smith's pedantic if not incoherant rants and screams (many of which takes a prismatic literary mastery and Artaud like insanity to understand.) Some hate the man and his music--just as well. He probably has the same disdain for them.  
Then The Fall fans can sometimes be subdivided between the pre-Brix era and the Brix-era (I like both, and have little experience with the POST-Brix.) The pre-Brix era can be defined by the "skrankin'" guitars, right-on bass lines and rock steady drums. More punk than not. Brix era stuff is more pop.  
"Smile" is an all out amazing song. The best track in my opinion is "Tempo House." Though monotonous and droning, it contains some of Smith's best stuff. This line sums it up for me as to why you would love or hate The Fall: "Snow on Easter Sunday....Jesus Christ in reverse. And I tell you the Dutch are weeping (in four languages at least.)
