[![](https://web.archive.org/web/20111010045020im_/http://3.bp.blogspot.com/_JdyrURA7JYI/S7phMl5EoLI/AAAAAAAAARI/-6EpdSKLLak/s400/maxbraxton.PNG)](https://web.archive.org/web/20111010045020/http://3.bp.blogspot.com/_JdyrURA7JYI/S7phMl5EoLI/AAAAAAAAARI/-6EpdSKLLak/s1600/maxbraxton.PNG)Yet more proof that Anthony Braxton is the motherfucking messiah. Bro just solos for almost 70 minutes on alto, soprano and bass saxophones. Also, one of the most acclaimed jazz drummers, Max Roach, fills in the rhythm and only adds to the more moody, and more chaotic moments. Each time Braxton grabs a new instrument, he creates new soundscapes; mixing his dexterous chord changes with his unique timbre that makes each instrument sound very interesting (he's probably the only guy who can make a bass saxophone sound cool.) Each solo crescendos and diminuendos with such grace, making truly "epic" improvisations. This was recorded live in 1979 and the lucky sons of bitches who heard it probably heard Braxton's most versatile and strongest performance (probably only rivaled, in my opinion, by his famed 1976 quartet performance in Dortmund, Germany.) This man is the reason you all have John Zorn, and this is quite obviously his inspiration.
