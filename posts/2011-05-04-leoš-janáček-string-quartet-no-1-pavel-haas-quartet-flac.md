[![](https://web.archive.org/web/20111010093904im_/http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-KCMga-GB5ao/TcG_RQ-YAoI/AAAAAAAAARw/OQDgni78JHI/s400/cover.jpg)](https://web.archive.org/web/20111010093904/http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-KCMga-GB5ao/TcG_RQ-YAoI/AAAAAAAAARw/OQDgni78JHI/s1600/cover.jpg)Leoš Janáček was an late 19th/early 20th century Czech composer who wrote two of the most emotive, gripping string quartets in the classical repertoire. Personally, these rank up there with Haydn's Op. 76 quartets as my absolute favorites from the genre. **If you download anything that I've just uploaded, download this.** The first bars of the Kreutzer will rip your heart to SHREDS. It would also behoove you to Wikipedia the Kreutzer and read its history and inspiration. Pretty interesting.

The extra Pavel Haas quartets (confusing that the ensemble name is the "Pavel Haas Quartet") are pretty good as well, but completely overshadow by Janáček's masterwork.
