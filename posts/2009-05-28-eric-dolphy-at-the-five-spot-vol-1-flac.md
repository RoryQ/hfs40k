[![](https://web.archive.org/web/20111008142551im_/http://3.bp.blogspot.com/_nmjS6fd9kfU/Sh7nTeGJm_I/AAAAAAAAABM/rpyH8bfiMa8/s400/folder.jpg)](https://web.archive.org/web/20111008142551/http://3.bp.blogspot.com/_nmjS6fd9kfU/Sh7nTeGJm_I/AAAAAAAAABM/rpyH8bfiMa8/s1600-h/folder.jpg)Booker Little was an amazingly gifted trumpeter who died much too early: at the age of 23 from kidney failure. Eric Dolphy was a gifted multi-instrumentalist who met a similarly tragic fate. Together, these two made some of the best post-bop/avant-garde jazz that I've heard to date. This is a live album taken from their stint at the titular Five Spot Cafe. Highly recommended.
