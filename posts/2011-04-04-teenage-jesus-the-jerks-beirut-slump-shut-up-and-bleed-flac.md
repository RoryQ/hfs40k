Prominent members of the late 70s "No-Wave" punk/performance art scene in NYC, Teenage Jesus and the Jerks and Beirut Slump were Lydia Lunch's first forays into the DYI music scene and remain among the best work of her long and circuitous career. Razor sharp punk-inspired dissonance married to post-punk gloom, these songs were very much meant as middle-fingers to the NYC and UK punk scenes, which had slipped into lazy self-caricature. If your tastes run toward goth, punk and delicious nihilism, then you might want to lend this an ear. It is both historical and incendiary.
