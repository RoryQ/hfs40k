The film soundtrack can be a fantastic medium for artistic expression. While some purely seem like audio cuts from the film (*Tetsuo: Iron Man*), and some feel the need to stand as a comprehensive album with songs and a concept (everything by Clint Mansell), some feel self-assured enough to create a sort of atmosphere in which they can play around. Gestalt, it's own important and inseparable world. That is, the songs do not need to be stand-alone great, and the album does not require an identifiable concept. The songs are not traditional 'songs' and the concept is atmosphere. Occasionally the result can be something genuinely absurd (Lee Hazlewood's *[Cowboy in Sweden](https://web.archive.org/web/20111010082726/http://lix.in/-5cbad0)*), and from what I've heard, most of these are genuinely absurd. Nostalgic, sleazy and eerily confident.

Piccioni was involved in the death of a young woman who was reportedly drugged, taken to a mass-orgy, and later drowned. He got away with it because his dad was a politician. He started his music career off as a radio-pianist, and that influence shines through here. You have the obligatory sleaze/easy-listening/exotica sounds with an almost constant organ in the background or foreground and other pianos interlocking the sound. Also a mixture of bossa nova and smooth jazz. All the things you'd expect from the soundtrack to a sleaze flick from 1970.

Highly recommended.

Peter Thomas is a German composer, he apparently did lots of music for film and TV. This is a "compilation featuring 16 unreleased tracks drawn from scores for erotic movies in the 60s & 70s and later sampled by Pulp, Air and Royksopp" (amazon.com cd description, where else). This CD is very lounge-y and has lots of horns and strings and a few great vocalists (sorry, I don't know who any of them are). Here is a great quote from Peter Thomas himself to sum up the album:

> "This compilation is a certain kind of chic, nutty, bizarre, spheric, sinful-horny music out of the erotic soundscape of the acoustic-phonetic-hotline-lounge-party music universe."
