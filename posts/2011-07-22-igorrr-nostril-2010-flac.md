My latest musical obsession. Eccentric breakcore blending pleasantly obnoxious and glitchy drum programming with various genres, the focus being on baroque music and death metal influences. The result is something where choirs are as welcome as screams, harpsichords can team up with blast beats, and no tempo is wrong.  
  
The best thing is, it's not genre-hopping for the sake of genre-hopping or being "avant-garde", nothing here feels forced like that. This is not the sort of stuff where a theme is introduced for 5 seconds and then tossed away. Hell, most of the songs manage to be strangely catchy. It's more like some kind of an improvisational bricolage inside a not-so-sane man's head where every form of music neatly blends into a personal, distinguishable hybrid. The boundaries of genres aren't being *broken* in a sense, because wherever the inspiration comes from, they have clearly ceased to matter. With his musical group therapy, Igorrr invites you to update your mindset likewise.  
  
This is the Quasimodo of the 21st century pouring his life into an audio diary. Nostril's ugly and raw, but also honest and even beautiful, as long as you look at it from far away and squint a little. Igorrr's no monster, he's just misunderstood!
