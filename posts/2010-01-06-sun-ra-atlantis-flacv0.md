At first, it seems like a lo-fi, bebop album and then the tribal/psychedelic influences fly in. Side A is filled with a lot of heavy percussion moments, some keyboard funk, and a classic saxophone melody on "Mu" that lingers on my brain. Side B, being the 22-minute opus title track (recorded live in 1967), is probably my favorite music composition of all time. Sun Ra uses a "Solar Sound Organ" (aka a Gibson Kalamazoo organ) and a clavioline keyboard. The beginning has a little drum part and then mellows out as Sun Ra makes sounds reminiscent of an alien space-ship (if one existed.) Slowly, he wields multiple keyboards and begins an onslaught of layered organ drones that cascade off each other creating an almost "doomy" atmosphere. It sounds epic, apocalyptic, and isn't really "free jazz" (the man claimed that he planned every note performed on that piece.) This album is really a love-or-hate album, but I hope you guys love it, because I do.
