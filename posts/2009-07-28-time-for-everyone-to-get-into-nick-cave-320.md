Because I didn't have to do any work uploading or posting, I'll at least write a commentary.. maybe it'll help if you don't know where to start or something.

I suggest starting with The Boatman's Call if you're into sad music like Leonard Cohen or Tom Waits, or Murder Ballads if you want something funny and disturbing.

Murder Ballads has both beautiful soft moments ('The Kindness of Strangers', 'Henry Lee' (with PJ Harvey), 'Where the Wild Roses Grow'), and some more rowdy and enthusiastic songs ('Stagger Lee'). Regardless of the tone of the music though, it is all very bleak.

I personally think it's a timeless album, but if you're not convinced, get the Boatman's Call to at least secure Cave in your music library, the songs there are more introspective and moving (I think he wrote it when he got out of rehab or something).

From Her To Eternity is sweet because it has a Cohen cover. Get this if you like post punk, it's his first album under Nick Cave and the Bad Seeds (also check out the Birthday Party for ugly rockabilly inspired post punk/noise rock with fucked up lyrics)

Tender Pray is cool because it has 'the Mercy Seat' which is so fucking cool that the late Johnny Cash covered it.

The Good Son is an acquired taste I think. It's more for fans of the Boatman's Call side of things, being more mellow than say From Her To Eternity. It's underrated in my opinion, every song is great, 'The Ship Song' in particular which is as strangely familiar sounding on first listen as the Boatman's Call's 'Are You the One (That I've Been Waiting For)'. Henry's Dream is fun, 'Papa Won't Leave You, Henry' and 'I Had A Dream, Joe' are enthusiastic numbers, 'Straight To You' is the more mellow Nick Cave.

No More Shall We Part is brilliant. It's probably the one I've played most after Murder Ballads. It's lyrically moving like the Boatman's Call, but with some more epic moments where the piano ballads lift up and go insane. 'God is in the House' is funny.

Nocturama is average my opinion. It's the only Nick Cave album that I don't really like.

Abattoir Blues and the Lyre of Orpheus might actually be a better introduction to Nick Cave.. As far as I'm concerned, Nocturama didn't happen. So Nick Cave returns from a three year absence, sadly without Blixa Bargeld, but with a gospel choir instead. Weird that Blixa played on No More Shall We Part and not on this because this is hardly mellow. Start with Abattoir Blues for an enthusiastic Nick Cave... All I can say is that it sounds enthusiastic. Abattoir Blues is also an awesome name. The Lyre of Orpheus is a lot more mellow. Looking back, it mightn't be his best effort, but it is a good starting point, and a good place to return to if you've been listening to his other stuff and maybe want something slightly catchier and happier.
