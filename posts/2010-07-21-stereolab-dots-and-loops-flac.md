It is deceptive in that it's both easy and very pleasant to listen to, while harboring complexities just below the surface level that reward close listening. Even though this statement is quite cliché in the world of musical critique, in some cases it just simply holds true. This record is *perfect* for a feel-good, relaxing day. I used to play it all the time in my dorm room on the weekends after waking up at noon and just chillaxin'.

And of course there are the heady krautrock influences. The many flourishes on each track, be they horns, bells, xylophones, or simply the beautiful dual vocals of our two French-speaking songstresses Laetitia Sadler and Mary Hansen (RIP), Can in many cases add so much to the krauty base that you wouldn't even Neu they were influenced by zee Germans. Anyway this is getting rambly.
