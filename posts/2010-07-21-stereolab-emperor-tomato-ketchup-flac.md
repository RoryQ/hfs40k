[![](https://web.archive.org/web/20111010052233im_/http://2.bp.blogspot.com/_nmjS6fd9kfU/TEcC8J1oeAI/AAAAAAAAAMg/kL9DAMzuvXk/s400/folder.jpg)](https://web.archive.org/web/20111010052233/http://2.bp.blogspot.com/_nmjS6fd9kfU/TEcC8J1oeAI/AAAAAAAAAMg/kL9DAMzuvXk/s1600/folder.jpg)Commonly viewed as Stereolab's magnum opus, I find it to be tied in genius with *Dots and Loops*, with both having their different reasons for amazingness. Where *Dots and Loops* is loungier, *Emperor Tomato Ketchup* is more energetic, with more driving rhythms and such. The typical (and extremely unique) Stereolab hallmarks are all here. The sweetly delivered, (Socialist) French lyrics, the steady rhythms, and the amazing array of synths and musical textures. You can hear the Silver Apples, Young Marble Giants, Neu!, Suicide, Francoise Hardy, but also the distinctly Stereolab. So...the ketchup's great, hop in!
