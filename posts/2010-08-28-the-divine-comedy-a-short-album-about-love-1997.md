In an attempt to write a Scott Walker-ish mini-album released to coincide with Valentine's Day, Neil Hannon actually accomplished a lot more than he had intended, some fans and critics claiming it to be his finest release to date. It certainly does sound like a Scott Walker album, Hannon's croon is perfect and the 30 piece orchestra really add to the melodrama. But oh my! The lyrics! To me the album never sounds fully sarcastic, nor fully earnest (well, obviously never earnest). Nearly every song is hilarious and touching at the same time, full of punchlines put there to invert old love song clichés. *Everybody Knows (Except You)* and *If...* follow that formula the most but both become rather absurd in their own right. The joke goes so far that the song steps out of satire and comes out the other end, and that's where *Timewatching* is perfect. So it's never obviously cliché and never obviously not cliché because that'd be a cliché! It must be pretty hard writing genuinely good/affecting songs and then being able to craft them around ludicrous punchlines and coming out the other side with a simultaneously very Valentines and very un-Valentines Day sounding album, but Hannon nails it.
