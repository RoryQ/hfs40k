*"Heresie* continues Univers Zero's unkempt iteration of Rock: taking the secular implications long-held in rock, applying the arsonists touch -- explosions. Shattered squealing crescendos, chunks of mistrewn ambiguous melodies, ritualistic chanting; prevelant evil expressed as sound.  
  
Such an idea has been heard before: Romantic exploration, beyond the Classical consensus, brought us destructive force, each instrument drafted in the conspicuous compositional conspiracy; a war against order, the aftermath of vanquished calm; yes, ideas expressed before. Eventually "good" intentions were long forgotten. Composers began spouting elegies of destruction, woshipping Vile and her Wistfully Cruel Cohorts. Composers could no longer ignore it; Schoenberg declared it music; all the rest affirmed, and continue to affirm, their music as "a return", disguising the pathetic procrastinatory reaction of their motives.  
  
So we come past Rock, into the decay of Rock, and History repeats itself. This time with no scholars are around to scrutinize, they've long given up criticism, perhaps opting instead to spread their musical bias upon students? There's a small audience this time around, an audience delighted at surprises, who holds the prejudice of eclecticism, who willingly searched out this group of fucked up musicians because hey, at least they're honest.  
  
This is a band with no intentions to integrate dissonance and consonnance, they've gone beyond that. Recognising that this man-made division is artistically irrelevant, Univers Zero's output on *Heresie* becomes tonal-concomitant. To what? Relentless rhythms and deliberate silence, slithering lower octaves and foggy climaxes, leering into the abyss, the status-quo of sufferring, incessant declarations of slurred passages. During this jarring ear-assault we are not frightened by the symbiotic relation between each note (though i'm not denying that Univers Zero uses atonality very well), but where and when these notes will pop out.  
  
The primal rhythmic drive is key.  
  
...Oh who am I kidding:   
  
Secular-ism is the key!  
Hence the *Heresie*! "  
  
One of the darkest albums ever, and it was released on 1979!
