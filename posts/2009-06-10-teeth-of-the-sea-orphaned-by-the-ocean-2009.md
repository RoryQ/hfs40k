[![](https://web.archive.org/web/20111010084609im_/http://3.bp.blogspot.com/_X0HDv9LMCK8/SjACdTsrQGI/AAAAAAAAADM/TB6Jeu3C9Ug/s400/2064015.jpg)](https://web.archive.org/web/20111010084609/http://3.bp.blogspot.com/_X0HDv9LMCK8/SjACdTsrQGI/AAAAAAAAADM/TB6Jeu3C9Ug/s1600-h/2064015.jpg)You know, it's easy to describe an album with a few words describing genres found within it to give a general idea of what you're getting into, but with Teeth of the Sea's debut I have been scraping at my mind to figure out how to do that.  
  
I have heard many, many, genres of music and a plethora of artists within those genres, and I have to say this release is one of the most unique and unclassifiable things I have ever heard. The way the album flows makes me perceive it is a living, breathing, entity of its own. A general idea of what you're getting into I guess would be a post-rock/drone/tribal/sound-scape cluster-fuck in an opium den complete with brass instruments.  
  
In a sea of clones and derivative ideas, this album truly stands out to me and is undoubtedly one of the best albums of 2009 in my opinion; recommended.
