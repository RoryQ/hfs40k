[![](https://web.archive.org/web/20111010064952im_/http://1.bp.blogspot.com/_nmjS6fd9kfU/TFceMGFThPI/AAAAAAAAAM4/4Jkw92jDe34/s400/folder.jpg)](https://web.archive.org/web/20111010064952/http://1.bp.blogspot.com/_nmjS6fd9kfU/TFceMGFThPI/AAAAAAAAAM4/4Jkw92jDe34/s1600/folder.jpg)"This and Siah and Yeshua dapoED's "The Visualz Anthology" are stone cold hip-hop CLASSICS, up there with any alternative rap in the beginning of the 90's. The two MCs have such amazing flow (Breezly Brewin' gets a lot of credit, but Buddy Slim has basically the same amount of technical skill) that it's literally like they're FLOWING over the beat, akin to water cradling and caressing each and ever rock in a river bed, these guys are just all over the beat. And the lyrics? Social commentary is deftly mixed with braggadocio on all of these tracks. From the much-lauded "Clear Blue Skies" to the amazing "Troubleman", the topics that they move between are simply so varied and the rhymes so well-written that this album just breezes (pun intended) past you and you find yourself listening again and again and again.   
  
If you have ANY interest in mid-90's East Coast hip-hop (what I see as the best hip-hop), LISTEN TO THIS." -- My RYM review.
