Don't get me wrong. DNB beats can get so complex (not just fast) that your feet won't possibily follow your brain. But its greatest strength is how well it can morph with other musical styles and inspire subgenres like drill n bass, breakcore, etc. Hive's Devious Methods (1998) is one of the best dnb records I've heard and it's outstanding in its own way. Imagine DJ Shadow experimenting with dnb beats on a turntable while DJ Spooky mix it with illbient made from where-the-fuck-is-this-coming-from cult sci-fi movie samples. There's even a small trilogy at the end. Dope, dark, filthy and heavy. This definitely deserves much more attention than only 16 ratings and zero review on RYM, though its score does say something.
