A name that brings cheer into the heart of every fan of punky and progressive music. They're like the Blue Note of 80's/90's punk rawk, man. This? This is a math rock record with post-hardcore influences. Shifting time and angular riffs are both present here, both hallmarks of the math rock genre. But in contrast to masters of wank such as Don Caballero, Hella, Battles, and damn near every single latter day math rock out there, these guys are more able to use their technical chops to make interesting songs rather than virtuosity for the hell of it (yeah, I said it).

I know I just dissed Don Cab up there, but the best possible description of Faraquet's sound just dawned on me: a combination of Don Caballero and Unwound, but due to the fact that these guys were essentially contemporaries, it makes Faraquet much more original than using two bands as points of comparison would make them sound. I know I haven't said a word about the album per se, but that just makes it more mysterious, enticing you to download it. I mean, what are you afraid of? Can't stand to hear good music?
