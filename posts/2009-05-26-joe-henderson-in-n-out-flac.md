[![](https://web.archive.org/web/20111008154930im_/http://4.bp.blogspot.com/_nmjS6fd9kfU/ShwLcvGfVGI/AAAAAAAAAA0/TMjuEGt6k8c/s400/folder.jpg)](https://web.archive.org/web/20111008154930/http://4.bp.blogspot.com/_nmjS6fd9kfU/ShwLcvGfVGI/AAAAAAAAAA0/TMjuEGt6k8c/s1600-h/folder.jpg)Recorded earlier in 1964 (April) than Inner Urge (November), this album finds Joe with the duo of Tyner/Jones (who would enhance several Blue Note releases of the time with their formidable combined skill), along with underrated trumpeter Kenny Dorham (who played a part in discovering Joe), and bassist Richard Davis. With similarly challenging and angular compositions and improvisations akin to those found on Inner Urge, this release comes highly recommended if you're interested in the more cerebral side of jazz music.
