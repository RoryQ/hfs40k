And damn if Mould doesn't kill everyone else's future efforts with this. From the opening instrumental "Sunspots", which is just like melancholic nostalgia packed into three minutes, to the cello-featuring "Sinners and Their Repentances" and onwards, you've got yourself the rawly introspective lyrics and emotive voice of Bob Mould, some excellent guitar jangles, a lot of varied instrumental textures due to the aforementioned cello, and plenty of excellent hooks and songwriting. Really, the only thing that I could fault this record for is because apparently everyone in the 1990's thought it was so great they set out to copy it and play it on "modern alternative rock" radio 'til you got fucking sick at the sound of anything like it. But that's like faulting Beethoven for the overuse of his 5th or 9th symphonies. It just isn't fair. If Mould's work here is judged in and of itself, you'll find it more than fulfilling. -- Obi
