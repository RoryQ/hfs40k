McCoy Tyner. Elvin Jones. Reggie Workman. That's my review.

I WILL say, though, that I HATE comparisons to John Coltrane that many Shorter albums from this era garner simply due to him working with some of the same musicians. Shorter's compositions are radically different than Coltrane's (namely, they're actually compositions, as opposed to just scaffolding for "sheets of sound" and flurries of improvisation) in that they're twisting, winding, nuanced nuggets of brilliance. The minor key melodies, the swaying rhythms...*JuJu* is a stunningly appropriate title because the music recalls dark voodoo rituals with an ever-present yet never overwhelming sense of foreboding. Check out the opening saxophone riff on "Deluge" and see if you aren't chilled to your spine, but then wowed by its beauty.
