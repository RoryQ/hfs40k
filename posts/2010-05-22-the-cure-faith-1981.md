I only really care for two Cure albums- *Disintegration* and *Faith*.

I found a nice intro to a review in an AMG review, pretty much saying that the Cure have been and will be *darker* than in *Faith*, but not many albums out there are as morbidly *grey* as *Faith*. The Cure can be big and sad but then they are sweeping. *Faith* is stripped down and minimal, like much of their early work, and can hit hard because of it. Well, none of the songs hit hard, but the atmosphere is oppressive in the best way possible. Look at the damn track names: *All Cats Are Grey*, *The Funeral Party, The Drowning Man*...... Only *Doubt* is angry, and the lack of passion elsewhere is pretty disturbing. Not that it's not passionate, it's lyrically gorgeous and the arrangements are sublime, but my god it's.......................grey!

*The Funeral Party* is the best track. Nadja covered *Faith* so maybe they like it as much as I do.
