Anyone with a decent knowledge of jazz musicians has heard of Wayne Shorter, who is best known as a member of the fusion group Weather Report and played soprano saxophone on Miles Davis' acclaimed Bitches Brew album. Alan, Wayne's older brother, was not nearly as prolific, but is certainly a talent all his own. Here, he plays flugelhorn (and trumpet on "Rapids") and is joined by Gato Barbieri on tenor saxophone, Charlie Haden or Reggie Johnson on bass, and Rashied or Muhammad Ali on drums. This album is structured in a similar fashion to Ornette Coleman's early records, a trumpet (or in this case, flugelhorn)/saxophone/bass/drum quartet with simple, hard bop motifs and completely free soloing. Barbieri is the most radical on this record, reaching Arthur Doyle level screeches but also being very dexterous and versatile. Alan plays his foil, being more mellow and more focused on counterpoint. Overall, it's an above average post bop album and worth a listen.
