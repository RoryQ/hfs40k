Long time leecher, first time contributor. Kicking off my shares with one of the top albums on one of the top labels, [em:t Records](https://web.archive.org/web/20111010071511/http://www.emit.cc/). This is the 2008 remaster of the 1995 album by Gas, simply named after its catalogue number, 0095.   
  
Mind you, this is not Wolfgang Voigt's Gas, Mat Jarvis used that name first. Like all of the old releases on em:t, this one used to be a collector's item. However, Jarvis recently re-released it on his own label [Microscopics](https://web.archive.org/web/20111010071511/http://www.microscopics.co.uk/) along with new material.   
  
The music is detailed ambient techno with an underwater feel to it, the analogue instruments lending it a nostalgic touch. It's comprised of six longer tracks surrounded by short, playful interludes, one of which is supposedly a full track compressed into two seconds. The highlight of the album has got to be Microscopic, which creeps to the stage innocently enough but steadily builds up to fireworks of blissfully screaming synths. But that's not to say the quality doesn't remain consistently high throughout the album. The music never stands still, something is constantly changing or fading in/out. The production is top-notch as well, even involving faux 3D sound using a Roland Sound Space processor, as typical of em:t albums.  
  
I'm not going to target this to any group in particular as any attentive listener can get sucked in. Recommended as the soundtrack of your submarine trips to the bottom of the ocean.
