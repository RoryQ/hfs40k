A collection of the two 1983 releases *The Bad Seed EP* and *The Mutiny Sessions*, which are the final two releases for the band before Cave went off and made *From Her to Eternity*. If you're a fan of Nick Cave and haven't heard his pre-Bad Seeds career you're in for a treat. Especially if you're into difficult post-punk/no-wave/noise-rock. Notable fans of the band include members of My Bloody Valentine, Coil, the Jesus and Mary Chain, Scratch Acid, Melt-Banana, Dinosaur Jr, etc..

"Hands up who wants to die!" Cave screams (actually screams) on the first song *Sunny's Burning* and shit gets real. Crucial to the band's *uniqueness* is the rockabilly/free-jazz/blues inspired guitar of Rowland S. Howard who claims they just sounded like your average garage punk band before he turned up. While it sounds like a huge claim saying you're responsible for Nick Cave's uniqueness, a quick listen to his previous band, the Boys Next Door, is a good indication of just how important Howard was. He died December last year, RIP.
