First, they debuted with Street Horrrsing, their 2008 fuzztacular that received great acclaim and was produced by one of the bands heroes, John Cummings of the band Mogwai. Street Horrrsing melded chimey loops, synth arpeggios, and tribal percussion with loads of cascading waves of fuzz. "Sweet Love for Planet Earth" is what parents play their children to lull them to sleep IN HELL :O, creating the seemingly gentle loop of chimes, which in turn gets run over by a lawn mower, which then falls through an echo chamber while some asian guy goes "AUUUGH". Somehow this is all beautiful even though it screams "pretentious!" when really, it's the most minimal and expressive music I've heard in the past few years. They're certainly more sincere than those Gerogerigegege fags.

Tarot Sport (FLAC/v0)  
[![](https://web.archive.org/web/20111010081740im_/http://2.bp.blogspot.com/_JdyrURA7JYI/S5nCqeCtTjI/AAAAAAAAAPA/azEzbbmM1Bo/s400/tarotsport.PNG)](https://web.archive.org/web/20111010081740/http://2.bp.blogspot.com/_JdyrURA7JYI/S5nCqeCtTjI/AAAAAAAAAPA/azEzbbmM1Bo/s1600-h/tarotsport.PNG)Tarot Sport is where all the pussies who couldn't handle the middle section of Street Horrrsing run off to. In all truth, it's hardly different than their predecessor in composition style, but here there is less space and tribal elements and more techno flavor. The melodies and synth arpeggios are also more apparent too, making this a less difficult listen and almost danceable at some points. Highlights include the mid-section of "Olympians" where twinkly synthesizers are the closest thing to depicting the stars and the moon in musical form. "Space Mountain" has the post-rock crunch that Mogwai had always been striving for, but never came across, and this is all over a funky techno track. "Phantom Limb" is the duo successfully one-upping Black Dice's signature industrialesque mash-up stylings. Overall, it's a less demanding listen, but still keeps true to their mission of meeting fuzz with beauty.
