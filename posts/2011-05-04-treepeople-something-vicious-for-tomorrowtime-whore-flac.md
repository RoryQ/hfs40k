[![](https://web.archive.org/web/20111010093919im_/http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-PKGRWGv4VTE/TcIiXYZuzdI/AAAAAAAAASI/AfBALw3gexM/s400/folder.jpg)](https://web.archive.org/web/20111010093919/http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-PKGRWGv4VTE/TcIiXYZuzdI/AAAAAAAAASI/AfBALw3gexM/s1600/folder.jpg)One of the most absolutely frightening album covers ever, but it didn't prevent me from continuing my love affair with Doug Martsch. From the first riff of "Liquid Boy", you know what you're in for. Only the finest guitar-based flavorings and spices, brought to you courtesy of Mr. Martsch. This was pre-Built to Spill, but there's something about the wonderful, reckless abandon of this album that makes it perfect for summer. It strikes the perfect balance between simpler indie rock and the complex compositions and instrumentation found on his Built to Spill albums. And of course, Martsch's signature voice is present to guide you through the careening guitars.

Also, dat [Smiths cover.](https://web.archive.org/web/20111010093919/http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EAEnjWixoe0) He makes a Smiths song...good!
