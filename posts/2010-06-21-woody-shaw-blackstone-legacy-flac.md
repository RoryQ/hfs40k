[![](https://web.archive.org/web/20111010173341im_/http://2.bp.blogspot.com/_nmjS6fd9kfU/TB-TXG6kaUI/AAAAAAAAAI4/i3PfzarJeGQ/s400/folder.jpg)](https://web.archive.org/web/20111010173341/http://2.bp.blogspot.com/_nmjS6fd9kfU/TB-TXG6kaUI/AAAAAAAAAI4/i3PfzarJeGQ/s1600/folder.jpg)This album draws many comparisons to Miles Davis' seminal Bitches Brew, and for good reason. The same brooding, sinister feel and electro-rock-jazz mix can be found here, although I find myself listening to it more often than the aforementioned because of its conciseness. And for this reason, it is a beautiful album of its own. Bennie Maupin, the bass clarinetist/flautist featured on Bitches Brew, makes a return here, and to amazing effect. The bass clarinet is one of my favorite instruments, and its rich, reedy sound lends much to the atmosphere of these albums. If you like jazz or simply good music, you can't be disappointed.
