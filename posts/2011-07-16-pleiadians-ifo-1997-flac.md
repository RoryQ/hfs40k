Now, I know from experience that even the slightest mention of trance usually turns everyone's text green. I've been quite antagonistic toward it myself. But it's silly to dismiss an entire umbrella term covering various, sometimes contrasting styles. Goa trance happens to be one the most rewarding ones to listen to, dare I say the IDM of trance? I daren't, but it offers you no vocals, no buzzing saw leads, no self-parodying levels of indulgence. Throwing those out of the window leaves more room for heavy psychedelia and varying blends of sci-fi and mysticism, always oozing with style and composed confidence.   
  
Pleiadians is where goa peaked after a few years of evolution. While the above average speed, complexity and length reserved for progression can make I.F.O. almost exhausting on the first listen, in the end those qualities are what really taught me to like this sort of music.  
  
Thick, detailed layers pop into existence, intertwine with other ones, morph into something else, and eventually dissolve. It's quite mindblowing how there's constantly some knob being twiddled if you just pay attention. And yet they manage to keep up a consistently alien, organic, at times divine atmosphere - the sounds do come from machines, but it's as if there's nothing mechanical about them. The collage presented here seems meaningful and elaborately designed, almost like a sentient puzzle trying to communicate with you and get you to crack it.
