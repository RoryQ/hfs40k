Andrew Hill is my favorite jazz composer. Yes, Andrew Hill is just that good. I've listened to a lot of jazz, and no one pleases my ears more than Andrew, with his cerebral, twisting composed melodies and improvisations. From listening to the vast majority of his output, I can classify his albums into one of two general categories: large group sessions and smaller quartets/quinets. This is one of the latter, and this is important because Hill's role changes somewhat based upon which category he finds himself in for a given album.

In quintets like this one, he takes the lead more forcefully, playing the leads, delivering sublime solos, and comping along in a way that spurs his bandmates into playing adventurously. Speaking of bandmates: Charles Tolliver, man. I love this guy. I'll probably post the Mosaic Select box set I have of his stuff. His tone is reminiscent of Freddie Hubbard's: beautiful, piercing, keen, sharp. Along with Woody Shaw, the best trumpeter of the 70's. Joe Farrell is an excellent saxman, and one with whom I need to familiarize myself more. Billy Higgins is familiar from previous sessions, bringing the polyrhythmicism that I love. Victor Sproles is an unfamiliar name to me, although he holds his own extremely well here.

The compositions are mysterious, complex, and multifaceted, just what you'd expect from Andrew. My favorite is the lead-off, "Yellow Violet". Just amazing, amazing, amazing, amazing. The intertwining saxophone and trumpet lines are delivered languidly, so they hang in the air, ringing out. Hill frames these intentionally lazy proclamations with his trademark "weird" chords. Just listen to it, man.
