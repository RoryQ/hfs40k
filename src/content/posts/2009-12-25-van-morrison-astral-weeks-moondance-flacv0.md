Astral Weeks, one of my favorites, is a stream-of-conscious folk album where Van tells stories with vivid imagery while being backed up by the famous bassist Richard Davis (Eric Dolphy's Out to Lunch, Moondog's 1969 LP) as well as many other great musicians. Probably one of the most optimistic albums I have ever heard. Moondance is Van's return to pop music, but with more personality. Really, the album isn't too bad, but after that album he would continue to milk the same formula of blue-eyed soul pop until today. Most would consider these two albums his best.
