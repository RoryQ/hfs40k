Hey everyone, this is the first of what I hope to be many posts on here.  Been following this site religiously for about a year, and I've been wanting to share for a while. Uh, a little bit about me, I'm a college student, trying to get out of the state of Alabama as soon as possible. Pianist, majoring in Jazz, looking to have a career in Jazz, Hip/Trip-hop, Electronic Music or something similar.   
  
But enough about me, this post is about this album. The label calls it a "minialbum," but in my opinion, there's not a damn thing "mini" about it. Miho Hatori and Yuka Honda cram more variety into this record's 48 minutes than seems possible. They obviously know their shit; this record is equal parts Parliament-Funkadelic, A Tribe Called Quest and Coltrane. Miho's gorgeous childish voice serenades over "Sugar Water," has a little more attitude than you'd expect on "Know Your Chicken," and is simply hypnotic on the less structured tracks like "White Pepper Ice Cream." (My personal favorite on the album.)  
  
That isn't to take away from Yuka though. I'm constantly impressed by the creativity of her chord progressions and production. You can tell this group is not carried by one member over the other, but really is a labor of love from the two of them.   
  
And don't get me started on the samples.
