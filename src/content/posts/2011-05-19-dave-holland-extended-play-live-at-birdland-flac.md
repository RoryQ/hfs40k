I love this album. Dave Holland is just about the only artist ever associated with ECM that I can stand. They put out the worst, watered down, borderline smooth jazz drivel (but damn is it well recorded!) this side of Kenny G. But that's beside the point. Dave here, on the other hand, is *nothing* like that. His pedigree is extensive. A member of Miles' early 70's fusion bands (*Bitches Brew, In A Silent Way* type stuff), he's also played with Anthony Braxton, Sam Rivers, and other members of the jazz vanguard. So yeah, this guy knows his shit.

Speaking on the album in particular, remember when I said ECM could record the hell out of sessions? This album will STUN you if you listen to it on the right equipment. The tapping of drumsticks on each other. The pluck of the bass. The stab of a chord on the keyboard. It's all just so beautifully pumped into your ears; and all for the better, because you'll need to pay attention to all the wonderful interplay that takes place on these extended (hence the title) improvisations. The compositions themselves are wide-ranging, with my favorites being the more funky, percussive cuts, like "Prime Directive". And LISTEN to the goddamn upright bass and drum duo to open up "Metamorphos"! The crowd "oooohs", and you will too. Simply orgasmic. The rhythm section can really lay it down, but they can also get cerebral, and they switch between the two with stunning grace. I can keep on with the logorrhea, but you may as well just listen to the damn album.
