Requested by tolkkii months ago (hopefully you didn't have to find it elsewhere)

Quite difficult to describe, it's post-punk but it completely transcends that in the same way that his work with the Birthday Party did. His voice is *more* toned down but still maniacal, and in my opinion the restraint adds to the unease (he's more an insane crooner than a punk singer). Doom doom doom doom pain and misery but never done as well as Nick Cave did it here, on the first Nick Cave & the Bad Seeds album. The cover of Cohen's *Avalanche* is intense.
