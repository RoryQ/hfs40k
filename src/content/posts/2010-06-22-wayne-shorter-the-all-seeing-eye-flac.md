It's like a who's who of jazz at the time. Grachan Moncur III makes an excellent show here, and Wayne Shorter's brother Al contributes to the last -- and very sinister -- track, *Mephistopheles.* Overall, the album sets up an atmosphere of mystery and darkness, which makes for a very interesting listen. While some of Shorter's compositions here aren't as concise as those found on some of the other albums he churned out during this remarkable couple-year span of productivity on Blue Note, they certainly hold up as excellent machines of improvisation.
