OK so Sugar is basically Bob Mould's post Hü--erm, post-*that band* that he started up a little ways into his solo career as an outlet for his more Hü--uh...aggressive and punkier songs. With the cutting guitars of Hü--AGH. With the cutting guitars of his FORMER BAND for the most part diminished (albeit still present for effect in places), Sugar emphasizes the wonderful penchant for melodicism, as well as the interesting lyrics, that were heretofore hidden underneath all of the fuzz. I really can't recommend this album enough for fans of *that band.* "A Good Idea" will instantly bring to mind thoughts of Doolittle-era Pixies, mainly because the Pixies are a sort of tribute band to Hü--...yeah. Then there's "Hoover Dam", which opens up with Mission of Burma-esque tape-editing effects before it jumps into one of the best singalong choruses of all time. You don't even need to know what the lyrics are about, it's just so cathartic and college-rocky. It sounds dated in a *good* way, in that it perfectly embodies the spirit of the times like only the best bands can do. Anyway, listen to this. Recommend for all fans of...*yeah. --* Obi
