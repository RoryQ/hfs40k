As I've already gone over the merits of Wayne Shorter in the previous post, and this album was recorded just about 3 months after "Speak No Evil", it's really unnecessary to say much again. Notable in that it features McCoy Tyner, my favorite jazz pianist, in one of the many recordings that he made for Blue Note in the 60s as a sideman to a tenor saxophonist (such as Shorter or Henderson), which inevitably led to Coltrane comparisons. Don't make that mistake. Shorter stands on his own.
