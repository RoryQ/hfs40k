I don't know how much HFS followers like this kind of thing, but I'm posting it any way 'cause I've already posted it on my blog. This is Khanate's final album which is sad because it might be their most experimental. On the self-titled first release, I was bored by the repetitive ugly riffs, Khanate were another (albeit very good) doom band. *Things Viral* was interesting because the band opted for negative space over ugly dissonance. The results were obvious- often in any form of art, what the artist chooses *not to* show you is more affecting, frightening or beautiful than what they actually show. The negative spaces there were all of that. On *Clean Hands Go Foul*, these silences are filled with noisy guitar improvisation. One of the best things about improvisation is that it is fundamentally sentimental, an aural insight into the musician's thoughts or feelings. At times, the dramatic intensity of Khanate's sound can be undercut by vocalist Alan Dubin's over the top bleak lyrical imagery (in my opinion, of course). Here, however, the music makes up for the extravagance, the painfully slow tempos and guitar improvisation creating a sort of intensity only hinted at in the *Things Viral*'s silent passages..
