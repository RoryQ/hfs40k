The wonderful, sweeping Romanticism of the theme, most prominent in the opening and closing movements, is said to have been inspired by Elgar's reaction to the horrors of World War I. I hadn't taken much to Elgar's music that I had heard previously (the Enigma variations along with his violin concerto and Symphony No. 1), but this smacked me in the face so hard I HAD to immediately share it. That theme is a thing of ridiculous beauty.

Du Pre's cello work, especially in the opening movement, will literally wrench your heart out of your chest.
