[![](/hfs40k/images/a1553b38786b813c81c18bb7be25d51b.webp)](https://web.archive.org/web/20111010173210/http://1.bp.blogspot.com/_nmjS6fd9kfU/TCE6AazQ-AI/AAAAAAAAAJA/BKcKTrHviKo/s1600/folder.jpg)An outstanding example of the Blue Note avant-garde of the 60's, we find little-heralded trombonist Grachan Moncur III recruiting some familiar Blue Note stars to push the envelope yet again. Percussionist Tony Williams', vibraphonist Bobby Hutcherson's and alto saxophonist Jackie McLean's presences shouldn't be surprising here given their penchants for exploring the "outside" of jazz on their own releases, but...Lee Morgan? Yes, and he contributes wonderfully to the off-kilter and haunting nature of this record. If the musical embodiment of a ghostly circus piques your interest, then it would serve you well to dive headfirst into this delicious album. Recommended to me by my high school history professor, who I still talk to regularly. I mean, the guy essentially owns me at everything music related. He's met my favorite musicians, worked at my favorite record store, and been in many a band. I thank him for this one.
