[![](/holyfuckingshit40000/images/eb70e21488869142b1bdd10bcc1e419e.webp)](https://web.archive.org/web/20111008155604/http://3.bp.blogspot.com/_nmjS6fd9kfU/Sg9b6ABlVdI/AAAAAAAAAAU/KgHx7Yy7OhY/s1600-h/folder.jpg)Another one of my favorite records, this is a live album recorded during the late 70s at the tail end of Dexter's third and final phase, after his return from his self-imposed European exile in the late 60s and 70s (where more traditional jazz was held in more respect than in the more fusion-leaning U.S. at that time). I particularly enjoy this album because it finds Dexter with more room to stretch out and breathe on each of the tracks, with the shortest actual song clocking in at twelve-and-a-half minutes. Not only that, but the tenor duel between himself and Johnny Griffin on "Blues Up and Down" is one of the most entertaining I've ever heard. Highly recommended.
