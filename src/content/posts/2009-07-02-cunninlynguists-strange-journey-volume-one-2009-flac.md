Last of the Cunnin' spotlight until Vol 2 comes out.  
  
It takes both talent and cojones to sculpt outtakes into some sort of concept album, and even moreso to make it into two albums, with extra remix discs and everything, and to make it all work requires a special sort of genius. So it is with CunninLynguists *Strange Journey*, a THC-fueled trip through the South in a run-down old hippie van. The sound is classic soul and reggae beats powered up, and it's a fun, cheerful sort of album, even when it's dealing with some serious issues. There's a bit of excess silliness, poop jokes and the like, but it's all in stride, the feel of hanging with old friends on a road trip. Live tracks weave well into the texture, there's a touring band mood to it, and their energy carries it all forward. Using a sample from *Koyaanisqatsi* was particularly inspired.
