When the first group of explorers was dispatched to investigate the remains of a large city on the desolate planet, one thing in particular caught their attention. For an isolated, ancient and supposedly long extinct society, they seemed to have been surprisingly advanced. It was unknown what had happened to the inhabitants, but all the towering, interconnected architecture was preserved as if in a time capsule. Even the ubiquitous yet identical table-sized machines were not only operational but also tediously processing something. Research of their design and functions is still inconclusive.  
  
Inside the hollow spherical building looming in the middle of the others, one of these machines was producing sound, breaking the otherwise ominous silence of the dead metropolis. It was a recording of a single inhuman voice monotonously talking in an unrecognized language. Every ten minutes there would be a short pause and the message would loop again.  
  
But once the recording was different. It was still the same metallic voice, but the pitch was higher and the pacing more rapid. It only lasted for a fraction of the original message before the lighting of the building changed from dim white to deep blue. What followed was music, the first compelling insight to the traditions of this mysterious culture.
