[![](/hfs40k/images/963eda7d8cd9746d9c9831e7d57e4a32.webp)](/hfs40k/images/a5390a05501e4ec2671084665a9c44c0.webp)More reminiscent of a hard bop album than anything Shorter had recently put out, this album finds Shorter within a sextet accompanied by a trombonist and an alto saxophonist, which provides one of the best jazz composers of all time with an excellent textural pallette with which to paint his musical ideas. Look no further than the opening bars of "Tom Thumb" to hear how the horns wind together around each other in a combination of sounds not frequently heard together on jazz albums. And on "Kryptonite", Spaulding (who steals the show on this album, in my opinion, with his piercing tone and blistering solos) busts out his trusty flute to add even more variety to the proceedings. A great set of hard bop influenced post-bopping.
