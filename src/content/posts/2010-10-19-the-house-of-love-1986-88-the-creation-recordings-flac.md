"Product Description  
23 track compilation combines for the first time all the UK indie act's Creation era recordings together on one CD, their 1988 self titled debut, The House of Love, a rare German album & bonus B-sides. Tracks, 'Christine', 'Hope', 'Road', 'Sulphur', 'Man To Child', 'Salome', 'Love In A Car', 'Happy', 'Fisherman's Tale', 'Touch Me', 'Shine On', 'Plastic', 'Love', 'Real Animal', 'The Hedonist', 'Flow', 'Nothing To Me', 'Welt', 'On The Hill', 'Loneliness Is A Gun', 'Destroy The Heart', 'Blind' & 'Mr. Jo'. 2001 release."  
Yeah, 1st lp + early EP/singles, this is pretty much all you need of this band. Guy Chadwick/Terry Bickers is probably the most underrated songwriting duo of all time (think about Morrissey/Johnny Marr, Ian Brown/John Squire, Brett Anderson/Bernard Butler, Mick Jagger/Keith Richards, John Lennon/Paul McCartney, ....). The Brits do have a tradition of producing such great partnerships don't they? Like many of those duos, after Terry left the band in 1989 to form Levitation, HoL just became more and more irrelevant. When they were together, they penned some of the (musically and lyrically) best jangly guitar pop of that era, with subtle hints at shoegaze here and there. Truly essential stuff.
