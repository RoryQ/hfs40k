Double Sunrise Over Neptune is a live album from the 12th Vision Festival in NYC, having Parker not play his main instrument (double bass, he passes the duties to a WHITE CHICK :o) but instead playing some unique horn instruments. The album is three long improvisations and a short intermission performed by over a dozen people. Two drummers, a bassist and guitarist are the bed for which a multitude of horns (baritone/tenor/alto sax, oud, trumpet) play in near-perfect unison. Like in famed jazz critic Piero Scaruffi's description of Mingus' The Black Saint and the Sinner Lady, each member only adds layers and colors to each piece with the "roots being the rhythm and the improvisations being the ripening fruit on a tree" that is JAZZ (Parker's liner notes for the album.) Anyway, it's probably the only jazz album of the decade so far that deserves the title of "classic." Free jazz veterans should enjoy this one.
