Out of the two volumes, this one's the actual "ambient" album. The packaging features a puzzle which involves matching symbols with blurry photographs. Blue Calx is the only track whose name is spelled out, the rest have nothing but those photographs for identification. For maximum scrobblability however, I've tagged these files with the generally accepted titles. But I went and scanned the art as well so you can check it out for yourself, here's an [Imgur album](https://web.archive.org/web/20111010135339/http://imgur.com/a/EDKhh) with six hi-res shots.   
  
Unlike the usual 20-minute humming drone pieces that consciously try to break free from structure, there seem to be self-imposed grids on SAW II. This gives the music a very memorable and specific nature, so that each track feels like an audio photograph of some scene. It's entry level and exit level at the same time: while it's a great introduction to the genre, it's something you'll keep revisiting even after delving into 70's Transylvanian horror drone releases with 100 cassette copies. If nothing else, it's a reminder of Richard's versatility: right next year he was to release the wacky [Hangable Auto Bulb](https://web.archive.org/web/20111010135339/http://holyfuckingshit40000.blogspot.com/2010/12/afx-hangable-auto-bulb-1995-flac.html) EPs.
