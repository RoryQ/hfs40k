Maybe you think it's hard to listen to. Maybe you think it's pretentious. Maybe you simply don't like it. Whatever your thoughts, you OWE it to yourself to sample a slice of this beautiful 20th century pie. *Peter and the Wolf* and *A Young Person's Guide to the Orchestra* are pieces essentially composed to appeal to a wide audience and attempt to foster a love of not only classical music, but the art form in general. And with performances like this from Previn and the RPO, damn if they don't do a bang up job of it.

My favorite piece of the two is most certainly *Peter and the Wolf*. Each character in the sparsely, but adequately, narrated story is represented by a different instrument or group of instruments in the orchestra. The bird, by the flute; the cat, by the clarinet; the duck, by the oboe; the grandfather, by the bassoon, etc. The reason why this worked so well for me is because of the exquisite recording quality. The beautiful sounds of all of these instruments and the leitmotifs written for each character seem to spring out of my headphones. Simply beautiful, stunning stuff. The story is simple, but you'll be enraptured by this simplicity, and then completely won over by the orchestration. Just listen.
