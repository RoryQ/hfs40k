"It seems most people tend to hold the viewpoint that this record, the last, is where Fly Pan Am finally broke away from their association with Godspeed You! Black Emperor and established themselves as their own entity. Fortunately, I don't subscribe to that idea because it's a bit sad. Granted Fly Pan Am has always contained some connection to Roger Tellier-Craig's other band, but they've always been strongly rooted in the insistent rhythms of dub and krautrock rather than Godspeed's punk styled orchestration.  
  
Like all of the band's preceding material, *N'écoutez pas* relies heavily on repetition to form the structures of its "songs." What's different here, though, is that the quotes around the word "songs" are no longer required, as the band engages in all sorts of more concisely structured works that are led by vocals: shouted, screamed, whispered, all in pidgin French. Framing the songs are rhythmic pulses of bass and drums while the guitars form angular, wiry patterns and a hail of noise and synth add to the mix.  
  
The songs range from cheesy, 80s-inspired dance tracks ("Brûlez suivant, suivante!") to fiery anthems ("Pas à pas step until") to long-burning pieces that descend into hazy noise ("Au tant zig-zag"). The actual songs are linked through a number of tracks that are more appropriately thought of as sound collages. Mixtures of live jams, samples and field recordings, these sound collages give a weight to the album and help space out the longer, more focused numbers.  
  
In a lot of ways, this album is similar to those released by Set Fire to Flames. While half the tracks here stand quite magnificently on their own, the others are existent for the album's cohesion and completeness. And because of this, it's a remarkably solid work as a whole. It's easy to get lost in its groovier moments while the noise and space aspects will give your brain a workout. The fact that nothing else out there sounds quite the same makes it even better. A truly unique and important work."
