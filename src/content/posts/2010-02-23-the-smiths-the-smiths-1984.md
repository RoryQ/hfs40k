I'm a *[Smiths](https://web.archive.org/web/20111010160227/http://lix.in/-74f41a)* fan, but not a Smiths fan, the italics indicating a 'title', I think. I hate *Meat is Murder*, *the Queen is Dead* and *Strangeways*, but really dig the 1984 self-titled album and b-sides/Peel Sessions collection, *Hatful of Hollow*. I always saw the post-*[Smiths](https://web.archive.org/web/20111010160227/http://lix.in/-78015c)* recordings as jangly, enthusiastic and catchy, which is probably why they're more popular. The opener *Reel Around the Fountain* says everything I like about the album, Morrissey's wailing about some sort of twisted love with his strange vocals and a repetitive melancholic guitar line provided by Johnny Marr. To be honest, the mid-part of the album does get slightly more enthusiastic, and I might hold that against it if I didn't totally dig Morrissey's slacker sentiment "I never had a job because I never wanted one" and strange last-minute insults "And did I ever tell you by the way, I never did like your face", or admire the way that the album 'cools down' into *Reel Around the Fountain* type material during and after *Hand in Glove*, *I Don't Owe You Anything* and the unsettlingly bleak *Suffer Little Children* being among my favourite Smiths-moments. Same formula: Morrissey's confident and surprisingly great lyrics/vocals, minimal drums and repetitive gliding guitar lines.
