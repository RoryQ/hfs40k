Released in '96, this album purportedly gave the boys in Mogwai the inspiration to start picking their way through the post-rocks on the shore of musical greatness. And a short-term member in Brendan O'Hare when the Ponies dissolved and Brendan descended from the drum kit to do whatever he did on Young Team (apart from the apocryphal tale of disrespect at an Arab Strap concert by incessant chattering, supposedly getting him dismissed from the group). Voices derives its excellence not just from its intensity, mood, and musicianship, but from its variety.  
  
I'd love to ask the band members about the genesis of the title track. I can't get over the similarities between it and "What Reason Could I Give" from Ornette Coleman's Science Fiction; rapid percussion contrasted with vocals in a slower meter. That and the fact that the intro is titled "Bells for Albert Ayler". "Last Outpost" and "Shizuka" provide a one-two punch - the former from David Keenan's subterranean/chanty vocals and the dramatic, cyclical yet structured guitar work at the climax, the latter from Rachel Devine's ethereal vocal movement through the haze into a widescreen blast of beauty. "Aegis Falling" channels AMM-experimentalism, most likely through Sonic Youth. "Sail Her On" and "The Fall of Little Summer" do take their cues from Nick Cave, but they sandwich "A Feather on the Breath" with its half-song-long guitar solo, a stunner which raises the bar on fret-wankery. "Does Your Heart Have Wings?" once again does the drama, but with every listen I've caught something new in the mix. "Song of Ansuz" goes for minimal folk on a tortured route and "La Vienna" ends it with one of those dark, warped courses where a chorus of souls seems too buried in the mix to escape.  
  
Innovative and filled with (vaguely restrained) emotion, it sticks in your neural pathways. So purchase this to place alongside your copy of Young Team to complete your mid-'90s really, really important post-rock collection, and you can play rock family trees with the drummer from Teenage Fanclub.
