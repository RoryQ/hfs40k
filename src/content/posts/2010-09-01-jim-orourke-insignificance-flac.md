As mentioned previously in my blurb about *Halfway to a Threeway*, O'Rourke got *nasty* here. Someone pissed him off. And so, we have O'Rourke's hard-rockin'-est album, filled with tunes that would seem happy were it not for the ironically venomous lyrics.

These two lines, from "Memory Lane", are all that really needs to be said:

"It's quite a gamble to speak out of place/these things could kill you, but so could your face." "Looking at you reminds me of looking at the sun/and how the blind are so damn lucky."

And of course, being the chameleon that he is, none of the American Primitivist nor avant-pop trappings of yore are really to be seen here. A lot of nice up-tempo rockers with that certain "it" to distinguish them from the rest that only Jimmy can bring.
