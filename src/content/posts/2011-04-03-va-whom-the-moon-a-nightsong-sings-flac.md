Since early 2009 I've been visiting this site of yours. This blog has been hugely involved in the discovery of many of what are now my favorite bands, so I really appreciate the chance I've been given.

For my first post, here's my favorite compilation album. It's a 2CD collection of neofolk/dark folk music from mostly Finnish bands and others from Europe. All of the bands on this album I believe have other works worth listening to(of course that’s subjective) and I’ll be posting various things from their discographies in the coming days.

~ The opening paragraph of the compilation’s digipack:

"Romantic art and poetry are informed by a couple of recurring motifs: nature, night, the primacy of emotion over reason, magic and mysticism. The Romantics were turning their backs on an increasingly enlightened and secular world, seeking refuge in virgin nature, to which they ascribed a soul, and in the world of fairy-tales and myths, of primordial and simple things. They worshipped the night, for it sharpened the senses and inspired the imagination. The night was the setting for the coming-to-consciousness of one’s own realm of emotions and for a journey inward, toward the individual’s sensations.”

I didn’t include the second and third paragraph because it obsessively recognizes the bands on the compilation album. The person having written the introduction being Stefan Belda of Prophecy Productions which is the label that released the compilation as well as what many of the bands that participated in the compilation are on (Nest, Les Discrets, Empyrium). So, I didn't feel like sharing that circle-jerking.

A quote from Empyrium about their song *The Days Before the Fall* on the compilation: “As with everything especially beaituful – it won’t last, but eventually it will return.”
