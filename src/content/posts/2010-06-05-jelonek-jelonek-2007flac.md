"Polish instrumentalist and composer Michal Jelonek provides a very pleasant surprise with this album, which was released in 2007. Jelonek is a multi-awarded musician and he has taken part in many musical acts an has composed music for several movies and commercial clips. As it seems, metal and rock is another passion he has, apart from classical music. So the marriage of his passions is portrayed in this special album. And I do mean special, because in a sub-genre that has exhibited many great albums, but many flops as well, it is hard to compete the former and to avoid be the later. Jelonek however, together with his friends, has composed an album that it is easy to follow, with a sincere and established approach to classical music and with a light progressive scent. Due to lack of vocals one would refer immediately to Apocalyptica. Yes, that's true. But I think that his music is closer to Rage's classical adventures and Therion. To be more exact, I would seriously consider Haggard as an important constituent of their sound as they are the closest we have to some heavy baroque influences in Jelonek's first album. Regardless of the influences and a couple of weak tracks like "Akka" and "Lorr", Jelonek's self-titled album is full of excellent songs the opening "BaRock", "B.East", "A Funeral of a Provincial Vampire" and "Machine Hat. Well produced and nicely covered by an artwork that has its symbolisms this is an album that every fan of the classical music/heavy metal will enjoy."[[metalperspective](https://web.archive.org/web/20100621002615/http://www.metalperspective.com/reviews/jelonek.php)]
