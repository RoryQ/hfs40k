This fuckery has no bearing on the music herein, except that maybe for once it was a proper indicator of the appeal. Its some daunting stuff. "Zurvan" seems to be quite a cloying bit, coming off to me (imho, of course of course) as bland, tired, and of good intent. I won't condemn. "Smashana" makes far more sense, touching on abrasion n travelling nearly faultless thru a wasteland, spiraling into depravity. Its almost a bit too overt for something without purpose beyond achieving itself. If that makes sense. "Mahvidya" - thats what it was all about anyway, right? It alone nearly warrants another half star, really. It takes the best of the current folk scene, n the best of Sun City Girls, n applies it across twenty five minutes. Its one of those dream tracks you n the mates propose during early morning soberings, still half drunk. That it comes to fruition in all its humble glory - that is worth writing about.
