The cliché "everything but the kitchen sink" couldn't apply to a more deserving band. On *Crazy Rhythms*, The Feelies (who Rivers Cuomo of Weezer apparently liked just a little bit, judging by the cover...) develop their unique take on the nervous, experimental style of music that was early 80's post-punk. This was one of the best periods of experimentation and advancement in rock music as far as I'm concerned, and The Feelies carved out their own niche amongst the many excellent bands of the time.

From their innovative use of a "clean" guitar style plugged directly into the mixing console to the insane drummer's closet of percussion employed to the krautrocking jams (best exemplified by my favorite track, "Forces at Work") to the nervous, uneasy vocal style that at once brings to mind such contemporaries as Tom Verlaine, David Byrne and Ian Curtis all wrapped into one, The Feelies embody everything there is to love about post-punk.
