Symphony No. 6, the Pastoral, is absolute melodic bliss. That isn't to say that Beethoven is all melody and no musical development here, oh no. He isn't renowned as one of the best composers of all time for nothing. Even as his more famous fifth and ninth symphonies have become somewhat laborious listens -- at least for myself -- due to their overexposure (and not at all due to any shortcomings on their part), the sixth remains fresh and beautiful, as if it had never been touched by anything that could mar its perfection. I have no clue why Bernstein remains dominant as a conductor in my collection, but he shows here why he was one of the most highly regarded of the 20th century with a delicate treatment of this ever-so-beautiful symphony.  
  
I can't explain enough how wonderful it is to listen to this in the morning, watching the sun rise in a beautiful blue sky as the verdant green expanses in front of my dormitory window sparkle; the morning dew catching the sun's first, tentative rays. Or, one can listen to it as one of Florida's signature afternoon thunderstorms rolls in (even during the "winter" here), as the churning basses that signal the same in the symphony rumble on. Beautiful stuff.
