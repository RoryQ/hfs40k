Karlheinz Stockhausen was a German composer who was recognized as one of the most important composers of the twentieth and early twenty-first centuries. Stockhausen's success was largely due to his groundbreaking work in electronic music. In addition his work has greatly influenced composers of art music, as well as jazz and pop artists. His compositions have been known to avoid the traditional methods in the composition of musical pieces. While, Stockhausen is commonly associated with the field of electronic music, he was also known to perform live performances that used solo instruments, chamber music, choral and orchestral music. With numerous notable compositions throughout his career, choosing a work to analyze can be a tough one.  
Included is an analysis of parts of the composition: Kontakte composed by Karlheinz Stockhausen. The composition was created in the late 1950s in Cologne, Germany with the assistance of Gottfried Michael Koeng, a German composer. Kontakte, which literally means "contact", was created through the combination of various unique techniques discussed below. While the work exists in two forms, as a complete electronic version as well as a second version that uses piano and percussion, the focus here is on the electronic one. The composition itself was produced with the help of an impulse generator. The impulses itself could vary in speed continuously between 16 and 1/16 per second.   
As far as the composition is concerned there is a great deal of evolution that takes place as it progresses. The composition starts off with intergalactic like sounds that are most commonly associated with Star Wars films. However it quickly transitions to a sound that gives the impression of black holes and evil like beings. The intermittent sounds present a very unique experience that almost gives the impression of galactic battle scene. The use of dead space (2:18) in the composition gives off a feeling of emptiness, commonly witnessed in horror films. Kontakte quickly transitions (3:55) with vibration like sounds that echo from afar, almost as if reinforcement is coming. The use of panning (4:40) allows the listener to hear a story unfolding that tells of an arrival of Martians to unlikeliest of places, Earth. Around the six-minute mark electronic sounds are presented that present the listener of an almost barren environment that the visitors (aliens) have arrived to. Suddenly the composition takes a turn with various piercing sounds almost as if plates and garbage can lids were colliding (7:32).  
All in all the composition Kontakte by Stockhausen is a pure and a groundbreaking demonstration of electronic music. The piece allows for "total surrealism" to come into play with the progression from a quiet to loud composition, with the use many techniques. Some of these techniques being: a snarling timbre, succession of pulses and an increasing reverberation. The use of various unorthodox techniques allowed for evolution of the composition, and its very unique and distinct sound.
