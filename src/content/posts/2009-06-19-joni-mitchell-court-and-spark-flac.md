One of Joni's best albums, which is a special distinction considering the amount of amazing albums in her unique discography. Melding jazz and pop into her folk background, Joni experimented on this album, creating songs that allowed her amazing vocals and extremely literate lyrics to be heard co-existing with the lush musical arrangements. While more accessible than later albums such as 1975's The Hissing of Summer Lawns or 1976's Hejira, you can still hear the individualism that separated her from the overwhelming amount of lame, soft-rock/radio pop singer-songwriters of the day.
