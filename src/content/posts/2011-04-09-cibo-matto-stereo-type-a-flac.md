I have to confess, as much as I love VIVA! La Woman, I've avoided this album for years. It has a lot to do with the addition of Sean Lennon on bass. I really dislike his father, and being an ignorant bastard, assumed he is the exact same person. I was very wrong.  
  
This album is fantastic. Their sound has really evolved from that urban trip-hop to a more shibuya-kei sound. The Trip is still there, but it's much more refined. Think a happy, upbeat Portishead. (Difficult, I know)  
  
I could point out the "great" tracks on here, but it's easier to list the bad ones. I don't care for Blue Train at all. I get what they're trying to do, but I'm not really interested in hearing pseudo-metal from my Trip-hop band.
