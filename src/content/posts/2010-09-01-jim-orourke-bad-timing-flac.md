While well-known for his works with bands ranging from Wilco to Sonic Youth, this mainstay of the Chicago avant-garde scene that gave us bands such as Tortoise, The Sea and the Cake, and Isotope 217 is one of my favorite singular musicians. From his work with David Grubbs in Gastr del Sol to his most recent album *The Visitor*, Mr. O'Rourke has continually expanded upon his artistic ideas in increasingly diverse ways.

1997's *Bad Timing* sees him during a period where he was at his most Fahey-influenced. Showing himself as a worthy disciple of the American Primitivism school of finger-picking masters, these four compositions are all around ten minutes long, allowing much time for themes to morph and meld into each other, transform, and double back in on themselves. It's amazing stuff, and it will definitely require your attention and demand more than a few listens to truly unfurl all of what's going on in it, although it's pleasant to listen to from the get-go, which is the hallmark of a great piece of music.
