"I'm not sure if this is as big a deal for a digital release as it might be in... you know, real life, but the artwork on Hammock's Maybe They Will Sing For Us Tomorrow was co-produced by Jonsi from Sigur Ros. Indeed, it was at the request of Jonsi and Alex Somers (who together form the duo Riceboy Sleeps) that these songs were written: the duo had an exhibition in Arizona, whose afterparty was set to mark Hammock's live debut, and it was for that very performance that these pieces were developed. Since that first performance the pieces have elaborated upon thanks to a little additional cello, but in essence the music supposedly remains the same. Regardless of backstory, these ethereal post-rock constructions sound rather special, occupying a weightless, dream-pop haze detached from the cumbrance of rhythm - it's just pure, effortlessly graceful soundscaping, made all the more impressive by the fact that it's almost entirely performed on guitars. You can certainly see why the Sigur Ros front man is so taken with Hammock: you'd be hard-pressed to find any music under the loose banner of post-rock as unreservedly beautiful and stately as 'Mono No Aware' and 'City In The Dust On My Window'. Highly recommended." - Boomkat
