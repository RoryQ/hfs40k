This album is astoundingly good. Dan Swano has successfully created a remarkable death metal album, the kind of death metal album with a sound that is actually a delight to hear (as opposed to brutal forms of the genre that are a delight to hear due to their complexity and power, not their rather "un-delightful" harsh sound). The keyboard melodies are very strong and carry much of the album, to the point that parts of this album seem like a collision between melodic death metal and synth-heavy eighties rock. However, where many other keyboard driven albums become painfully cheesy, Moontower becomes melodic - soaring and beautiful at its best moments. 'Beautiful' is not a word I would normally associate with death metal, but there are some truly beautiful moments here, such as the end of Add Reality.  
  
This album can also boast some of the best death vocals of any. Swano's growls are not only full of power, but very distinct and comprehensible. His lyrics are often clear and the vocals do not clash or fail to work cohesively with the melodies, but often complement and progress them. There are clean vocals every now and then which are even more effective - it would perhaps be better if they were used more frequently, but that may dilute their effect, so the sparing use does have its perks. The most astounding aspect of this album is not how good it sounds, but that an album this mature and developed was recorded entirely by one individual.  
  
Besides being a pretty good album in its own right, it is also the kind of album that would be ideal to use to introduce someone to death metal. From this foundation, a prospective death metal fan could be eased into the genre rather than being put off by furious, crushing, brutal technical insanity. As far as death metal goes, this is comparatively welcoming. Maybe I have been desensitised, though.
