[![](/hfs40k/images/covers/2010-06-21-larry-young-unity-flac.webp)](/hfs40k/images/covers/2010-06-21-larry-young-unity-flac.webp)Larry Young played the organ. But, contrary to the prevailing role of the organ in jazz at the time (when it was even used), which was mainly as a replacement to the piano in soul jazz, Young could be described as the McCoy Tyner/Andrew Hill of the piano. Namely, he mixed the avant-garde with amazing compositional prowess. These aren't howling screechfests, rather, they are wonderfully crafted mediums for improvisation, like all good jazz pieces should be. If you've seen my posts on this blog before, you know I love me some Joe Henderson and Elvin Jones. So, combine these two with the excellent and visionary (and SUPREMELY underrated) trumpet of Woody Shaw, and you have four like-minded geniuses making amazing and unique music.
